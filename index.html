About Our Island

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano.
Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. However, very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially by the older residents. Until a recent increase in tourism, most of the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.
